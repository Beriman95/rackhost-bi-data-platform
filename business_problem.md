Business Context

A customer support operation generates large volumes of telephony and ticketing events daily. These events represent real customer demand, workload intensity, service quality, and operational risk. Prior to this product, these signals existed in fragmented operational systems without a unified analytical layer.

Core Problems Before the Product

No single source of truth for operational performance

Manual, delayed, and inconsistent reporting

No historical visibility into SLA behavior

Staffing decisions based on intuition instead of load data

No objective agent-level performance benchmarking

No audit-ready operational traceability

Business Risks Without Intervention

Systematic SLA breaches without early detection

Inefficient overstaffing or dangerous understaffing

Escalation bottlenecks invisible to management

Loss of institutional knowledge during staff turnover

Increasing support cost without measurable efficiency gain
